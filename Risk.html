⚠️ Understanding Risk

Every investment carries risk. Learn what they are and how to manage them like a smart investor.

Types of Risk
📉

Market Risk

The entire market can fall due to economic events, wars, or pandemics. Even good companies lose value when the whole market crashes. This affects everyone.

🏢

Company Risk

A specific company can perform badly due to poor management, scandals, or failed products — even when the rest of the market is doing well.

💧

Liquidity Risk

Sometimes you may not be able to sell your stock quickly at a fair price. This is common with small or obscure companies that have very few buyers.

💱

Currency Risk

If you invest in foreign stocks, changes in exchange rates can affect your returns — even if the stock price itself went up.

📰

News & Sentiment Risk

A single news headline can crash a stock overnight. Rumors, social media trends, and investor emotions can cause wild price swings with no logical reason.

🧠

Emotional Risk

The biggest risk is YOU. Panic selling during a crash, or greedily buying at peaks, are the most common reasons investors lose money.

How to Manage Risk
🥗

Diversify Your Portfolio

Don't put all your money in one stock. Spread it across different companies and sectors so one bad stock doesn't ruin everything.

📅

Invest for the Long Term

Short-term markets are unpredictable. But historically, markets have always recovered and grown over 10+ year periods. Be patient.

🔍

Do Your Research

Before buying any stock, study the company. Look at its profits, debt, products, and competitors. Never buy based on tips alone.

🧘

Control Your Emotions

Don't panic sell when markets dip. Don't greedily buy at all-time highs. Stick to your strategy and ignore short-term noise.

💸

Invest Regularly (SIP)

Instead of investing all at once, invest a fixed amount every month (called SIP). This reduces the impact of market highs and lows.

🛑

Set a Stop Loss

Decide in advance how much loss you can tolerate. If a stock drops 15–20%, sell it and cut your losses. Don't hope it will "come back."
