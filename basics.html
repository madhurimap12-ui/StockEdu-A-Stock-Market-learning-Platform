Key Concepts
🏢

What is a Stock?

A stock represents a small ownership piece of a company. When you buy a stock, you become a shareholder — meaning you own a tiny part of that business.

📊

What is a Stock Market?

A stock market is a place (physical or digital) where buyers and sellers trade stocks. Famous examples include BSE, NSE in India and NYSE, NASDAQ in the USA.

💰

How Do You Make Money?

You earn money in two ways: Capital Gains (selling a stock at a higher price than you bought it) and Dividends (a share of company profits paid to you).

🔄

What is Trading vs Investing?

Trading = buying and selling frequently for short-term profit. Investing = holding stocks for years to grow wealth slowly and steadily.

💡 Beginner Tip: Never invest money you can't afford to lose. Start by learning — you're already doing that right now! Most successful investors spend months studying before putting in a single rupee.
Deep Dives
What is a Share and a Shareholder? ▼
A company divides its ownership into millions of tiny equal pieces called shares. If a company has 1,000,000 shares and you buy 100, you own 0.01% of that company. That makes you a shareholder. Shareholders sometimes get voting rights on big company decisions.
What is an IPO (Initial Public Offering)? ▼
When a private company wants to raise money from the public, it "goes public" through an IPO. This is when the company first sells its shares on the stock market. Think of it as the company's debut on the stock exchange. After the IPO, anyone can buy or sell those shares.
What is a Bull Market vs a Bear Market? ▼
A Bull Market 🐂 is when stock prices are rising — investors are confident and optimistic. A Bear Market 🐻 is when prices are falling — usually by 20% or more — and investors are fearful. Both are normal parts of the market cycle. Long-term investors don't panic during bear markets.
What is Market Capitalisation (Market Cap)? ▼
Market Cap = Share Price × Total Number of Shares. It tells you the total value of a company in the market. Companies are grouped as Large Cap (very stable, e.g. Reliance, TCS), Mid Cap (moderate growth), and Small Cap (high risk, high reward).
