📂 Types of Stocks

Not all stocks are the same. Learn the different categories so you can make smarter investment decisions.

Main Categories
🏛️

Common Stock

The most typical type. Owners get voting rights and may receive dividends, but are last to be paid if the company goes bankrupt.

Medium Risk
⭐

Preferred Stock

Owners get paid dividends before common stockholders. Less voting rights but more stable income. Like a middle ground between stocks and bonds.

Lower Risk
🚀

Growth Stocks

Companies expected to grow faster than average — like tech startups. They rarely pay dividends; profits are reinvested to grow the business.

High Risk
💵

Dividend Stocks

Companies that regularly share profits with shareholders. Great for passive income. Usually older, stable companies like FMCG or utilities.

Lower Risk
🔵

Blue Chip Stocks

Stocks of large, well-established, financially stable companies with a long history. Examples: Reliance, TCS, HDFC Bank in India.

Lower Risk
📉

Penny Stocks

Very cheap stocks (often under ₹10) from small companies. Extremely high risk — prices can swing wildly. Not recommended for beginners.

Very High Risk
💡 Beginner Tip: As a beginner, stick to Blue Chip or Dividend Stocks. They are more stable and less likely to cause huge losses while you're still learning the market.
